* IRIS Observations of Short-term Variability in Moss Associated with 
  Transient Hot Coronal Loops (Testa et al. 2020)
  - https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2020ApJ...889..124T/abstract
  - high variability moss spectra observed by IRIS
  - Comparison with 1D RADYN simulation
     - E source : thermal conduction / nonthermal electrons
  - Mg II triplet can be an indicator of existance of nonthermal electrons
  - Transition region blueshift, Mg II triplet
    --> particle acceleration via small magnetic reconnection events.
  - limitation of models
    - relatively small Si IV nonthermal broadening in simulation (> 20 km/s)
       - missing physical processing
       - simultaneously different heating processes?
       - only single loop case / viewing angle? (perpendicular to the B fields?)
    - initial Si IV Doppler velocity = 0 km/s <--> observations
  - classification of events according to the energy sources.
    1. Strong evidence of nonthermal electrons. 
      - Mg II triplet emission & Si IV blueshift (4/10)
    2. Liely presence of nonthermal electrons.
      - small Si IV blueshift (3/10)
    3. Lack of evidence of nonthermal electrons (3/10)
    

* Investigating the Response of Loop Plasma to Nanoflare Heating Using RADYN Simulations
  (Polito et al. 2018)
  - https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2018ApJ...856..178P/abstract
  - 1D HD simulation, synthesized intensity and Doppler shift
  - Parameteres
    - Energy transport by nonthermal electron beaming, conduction
       - low-energy cutoff : 5, 10, 15 keV --> High energy can reach the chromopsphere
    - total energy : 10^24, 6*10^24, 10^25 erg
    - half-loop length : 15, 50, 100 Mm (20, 70, 140") --> determine density
    - Initial loop-top temperature : 1, 3, 5 Mk --> determine density
  - Si IV intensity : Mostly sudden increase, except high density
  - Si IV blueshift : if nonthermal electron can reach the chromosphere
    --> low density, high cutoff energy of nonthermal electron
  - AIA 94A light curve <--> Si IV intensity 
    --> comparing the contribution of energy release between Chorona and choromosphere


* Evidence of nonthermal particles in coronal loops heated impulsively by nanoflares
  (Testa el al. 2014)
  - https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2014Sci...346B.315T/abstract
  - IRIS obs. 
    - typical duration of 20-60s, Si IV upflow ~ 15 km/s 
  - RADYN simulation 
    - Require nonthermal electron beam, event duration < 30 s, total E ~ 10^24~24 erg
    - synthetic spectra : 
      - similar duration of the heating event
      - Si IV blueshift = Nonthermal electron beam / Redshift = thermal conduction
      - location of energy deposit depends on E_c (low energy cut-off) of electron beam, 
        and affects blue/redshift and brightenings of Ch / TR lines. 
  - importance of determination of E_c
    - drive the nonthermal energy content
    - constrain mechanisms of particle acceleration

* Plasma Diagnostics of Transition Region "Moss" using SOHO/CDS and TRACE 
  (Fletcher & De Pontieu 1999)
  - https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1999ApJ...520L.135F/abstract
  - Moss observation using SOHO CDS & TRACE 171 A
  - T ~ 0.6-1.5 MK / associated with T > 2MK hot loop
  - density ~ 2-5 * 10^9 cm^-3
  - filling factor ~ 0.1
